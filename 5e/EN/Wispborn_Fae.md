## Summary

Wispborn Fae — known among their own kind as **Syllae of the Wispergrove** — are sentient beings formed from condensed **mana**. Before gaining identity, they exist as drifting magical motes called **wisps**, luminous sparks of fey energy that wander through regions of intense magic.

Over decades or even centuries, a wisp slowly gathers memories, instincts, and personality. When its magical essence becomes stable enough, it undergoes a transformation known as **Condensation**, forming a permanent body and awakening as a **Wispborn**.

Though they often adopt small humanoid or spirit-like forms to interact with other creatures, their bodies are not truly biological. They are manifestations of living magic — delicate, radiant, and deeply connected to the flows of arcane energy that shape the planes.

Most Wispborn originate from places where magical currents gather naturally, particularly the **Wispergrove**, a legendary Feywild forest where new wisps constantly emerge from the surrounding mana. From there they spread across the Feywild and occasionally into the Material Plane, drawn by powerful magic, ancient sites, or simple curiosity.

Because of their innate sensitivity to magical forces, Wispborn have long served the Feywild as **observers, messengers, scholars, and guardians of arcane balance**. Many spend centuries exploring the nature of magic itself, following ley lines, studying magical phenomena, or protecting places where the boundaries between planes grow thin.

A Wispborn's life eventually ends in **Dissolution**, when their magical essence returns to the ambient mana currents from which they formed. Among their people this is not considered death, but rather a peaceful return to the greater flow of magic. Some Fey scholars believe fragments of memory from dissolved Wispborn linger within the mana currents, subtly influencing the personalities of newly forming wisps.

Though small in size, Wispborn are powerful manifestations of arcane life — curious, mystical, and forever tied to the living currents of magic that shape the cosmos.

Typical measures:

- **Average height:** 8–16 inches (20–40 cm)
- **Creature Type:** Fey
- **Lifespan:** potentially immortal

## What's known

Wispborn do not truly have a birthplace. Their earliest records claim they were first seen in the **Wispergrove**, a region of the Feywild where raw magical currents gather like mist over a lake.

When magic saturates the land for long enough, fragments of mana begin to swirl together, forming drifting lights known simply as **wisps**. These tiny entities are not yet people — they are instinctive echoes of magic, curious and playful but lacking identity.

Over decades, sometimes centuries, a wisp slowly accumulates memory and personality. If it survives long enough, it undergoes a transformation known among their kind as **Condensation**.

During Condensation the wisp compresses its magical essence into a stable form; becoming a **Wispborn**.

### Early History

Ancient Feywild chronicles describe the first Wispborn as **keepers of magical balance**. Their natural sensitivity to mana flows allowed them to sense distortions in the fabric of the Feywild long before other creatures could.

Because of this, many courts of the Feywild treated Wispborn as:

- Messengers
- Magical observers
- Guardians of unstable ley lines
- Interpreters of arcane disturbances

Over time some Wispborn left the Feywild entirely, following magical currents into the **Material Plane**, where they became scholars, tricksters, wandering spirits, or protectors of magical sites.

### The Wispergrove

The **Wispergrove** is believed to be the largest natural birthplace of Wispborn.

It is described as:

- A forest where the air hums with magic
- Glowing motes drifting between ancient trees
- Streams that shimmer with arcane energy

In this place, new wisps are born almost constantly.

Some Wispborn return there late in life to release their essence back into the mana currents, allowing new wisps to eventually form.

### Life Cycle

The life of a Wispborn typically follows four phases:

1. **Wisp Phase** – A drifting magical mote with no identity.
2. **Condensation** – The moment the wisp gains awareness and form.
3. **Awakened Life** – Centuries of exploration and experience.
4. **Dissolution** – The Wispborn returns its mana to the magical currents.

This cycle is not considered death among their people; rather, it is viewed as **returning to the great flow of magic**.

Although Wispborn do not age or die of natural causes, many eventually feel a deep instinct that their time of experience is complete. When this moment comes, they willingly enter **Dissolution**, allowing their essence to disperse back into the ambient currents of mana.

### Cultural Beliefs

Wispborn societies are often philosophical and mystical. Common beliefs include:

- Magic is a **living current**, not merely a tool.
- Balance between planes is essential for reality to remain stable.
- Curiosity is a sacred instinct.

Many Wispborn therefore become:

- Explorers
- Magical scholars
- Guardians of ancient sites
- Mischievous tricksters

### Rare Black Wisps

Occasionally a wisp forms with a **void-like coloration** with deep black or dark violet; these are sometimes called **Void Wisps**.

Legends claim they are born when:

- Planar boundaries weaken
- Ancient magic stirs
- The cosmos itself shifts

Some cultures treat them as **prophets**, others as **harbingers of change**. In some rare cases, the colors of a Wisp slowly begin shifting toward a void-like hue.
This transformation occurs gradually, and its cause remains unknown.

## Race Traits

As a Wispborn Fae, you have the following traits.

### Race & Size

Race: **Wispborn Fae**

Size: **Small**

Small creatures are smaller than Medium creatures and are
handled differently by some mechanical effects (grapples, carrying capacity,
and certain reach interactions). For play: you count as Small for any rule or
feature that checks creature size. Being Small emphasizes agility and subtlety
at the cost of raw physical might.

Wispborn typically stand between **8 and 16 inches tall**, though their magical nature allows their form to subtly shift over time.

### Speed
Your walking speed is **25 feet**.

You also have a **flying speed of 30 feet**, but you cannot fly while wearing heavy armor.

### Ability Score Increase
Your **Charisma score increases by 2**, and your **Dexterity score increases by 1**.

### Languages
You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **Sylvan**.
You also learn one additional language of your choice.

### Skills

#### Fey Sensitivity
Your body is made of condensed magical energy and naturally reacts to magical currents.

You can cast **Detect Magic** once without expending a spell slot.
You regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest.

Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this spell.

#### Mana Body
Your form is sustained by magical energy rather than physical biology.

You do not require food or drink, and instead absorb ambient magical energy during your rest.

You still require **sleep or trance normally**.

#### Frail Strength
Your body is delicate compared to larger creatures.

You have **disadvantage** on **Strength checks** and **Strength saving throws** when contesting a **creature that is larger than you**.

#### Elemental Affinity

Your magical nature resonates with elemental forces.

When creating your character, choose **one elemental affinity** from the Elemental Affinity Table.

Each affinity grants access to a unique list of spells. **Charisma** is your **spellcasting ability for these spells**.

> ###### Void Affinity
>
> The **Void affinity** represents unstable magical potential rather than a single element.
>
> Because Void has no spell list of its own, Void users draw power from other affinities.
>
> - At **1st level**, choose **one elemental affinity**.
> - At **3rd level**, choose a **second affinity**.
> - At **7th level**, choose a **third affinity**.
> - At **15th level**, choose a **fourth affinity**.
>
> You may learn spells from **any affinity you have chosen**.

##### **Affinity Spell Learning**

As you gain levels, you unlock the ability to learn spells from your elemental affinities.

These spells **do not count against the number of spells you know or prepare from your class**.

When you gain access to a new **spell level** in the Affinity Spell Slots table, you learn **one spell of that level** from one of your chosen affinities.

You may also learn **one additional affinity spell whenever your proficiency bonus increases**.

**Spell Swapping.**
Whenever you gain a level, you may replace **one affinity spell you know** with another spell of the **same level from the same affinity**.


##### **Affinity Spell Slots**

You gain special spell slots that can **only be used to cast spells from your Elemental Affinities**.

These spell slots are **separate from your class spell slots**.

You regain all expended **Affinity Spell Slots** when you finish a **long rest**.

##### **Casting Affinity Spells**

• Affinity spells use **Charisma** as your spellcasting ability.
• You may cast them **only** using Affinity Spell Slots **at first**. If you run out of Affinity Spell Slots, you may expend a normal spell slot of the same level or higher to cast an Affinity spell.


#### Flickering Veil

Your mana body can briefly disperse into drifting motes of arcane light.

As a **bonus action**, you shroud yourself in a flicker of fey magic, becoming **invisible until the start of your next turn**.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your **proficiency bonus**, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a **long rest**.

> ##### **Flickering Veil**
>
> *2nd-level illusion*
>
> **Casting Time:** 1 bonus action
> **Range:** Self
> **Components:** V, S
> **Duration:** Until the start of your next turn
>
> Your body dissolves into drifting particles of mana and faint faelight.
>
> You become **invisible until the start of your next turn**. Anything you are wearing or carrying becomes invisible with you.
>
> The invisibility ends early if you **make an attack**, **cast a spell**, or **force a creature to make a saving throw**.
>
> This effect represents your form briefly dispersing into arcane motes before reforming a moment later.

#### Scale Manipulation

Your mana form can alter the scale of creatures and objects.

Starting at **3rd level**, you can cast **Enlarge/Reduce** without expending a spell slot.

You can use this feature **twice per long rest**.

Starting at **5th level**, you can use this feature **four times per long rest**.

Charisma is your **spellcasting ability** for this spell.

When you cast the spell using this trait, you may target:

• **yourself**, and
• **one additional willing creature**, or
• **one nonmagical object**

within range.

---

> ##### **Enlarge/Reduce**
>
> *2nd-level transmutation*
>
> **Casting Time:** 1 action
> **Range:** 30 feet
> **Components:** V, S, M (a pinch of powdered iron)
> **Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 minute
>
> You alter the size of creatures or objects within range. Choose either **Enlarge** or **Reduce**.
>
> You may target **up to two creatures or objects you can see within range**, one of which may be **yourself**. Each target must be affected by the same option (Enlarge or Reduce).
>
> An **unwilling creature** can make a **Constitution saving throw**. On a successful save, the spell has no effect on that creature.
>
> If the target is an **object**, it must not be worn or carried.
>
> **Enlarge.**
> The target’s size increases by one category. The target also has **advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws**. If the target is a creature, its attacks deal **an extra 1d4 damage**.
>
> **Reduce.**
> The target’s size decreases by one category. The target has **disadvantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws**. If the target is a creature, its attacks deal **1d4 less damage** (minimum of 1 damage).
